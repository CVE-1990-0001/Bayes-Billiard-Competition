🎱 Bayes Billiard Competition

A Bayesian estimation game for two teams

Team Setup

How to play:
Each team has a billiard table (0–90 cm). A hidden white cue ball is placed at a random position. 5 red clue balls are thrown — you see only whether each landed LEFT, RIGHT, or HIT relative to the cue.
Extra clue ball cost increases separately per team (5, 10, 20, 40, ...).
The cue and clue-ball positions stay fixed across rounds so you can refine ranges progressively. Round 1 max range width is 60 cm, then it shrinks by 30% each round.
Correct ranges earn more budget when they are narrower; wrong ranges cost 30% of budget.
Guess the exact position to win +200 € and end the game!
